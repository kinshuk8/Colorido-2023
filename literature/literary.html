JAM, is something of a social experiment. Eight people are given a minute of fame. The same minute of fame. And only one person can speak at a time. And boy, do people HATE sharing their minute…
Once you do finally manage to start speaking, you can speak only so long as there is absolutely no deviation from coherent continuous (interesting) speech. And the first one to point out a flaw, will buzz you (with great panache) and grab the mic from you! As if it isn’t bad enough, we introduce another player into the fray… This player is called the JAM mod (among lot of other things), is around to make your life miserable, poke fun of you, give you your topics and moderate all objections. So be utterly bamboozled as you die laughing (or crying) as the incredible speakers of the queen’s English buzz it out, trying not to stutter, stammer, pause “unnaturally”, make any grammatical errors or repeating anything… And fail miserably…

Specific Rules

  • This is an Individual Event.
  • Topic will be given on the spot.
  • The time limit for preparation will be 2 min.
  • The time limit for presenting will be 1 min.
  • Only English Language is allowed

Image description conveys the equivalent information that a sighted reader would get when they look at a picture. It helps in recognising the tiny details of an image when seen in brief and so that the readers don’t miss out on them and improves observant skill of the person.

Specific Rules

Preliminary Round:
  • An image will be displayed on the screen and the participants should write down the description of the image.
  • The time limit is 20 mins.
  • Best participants will be shortlisted.
  • Required stationery is provided.

Stage Round

  • Each shortlisted candidate will be displayed a separate picture on spot.
  • The candidate can prepare for 60 secs.
  • The candidate should talk about the image describing it.
  • Time limit is 2 mins for each candidate.
